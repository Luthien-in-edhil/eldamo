[ᴹ]√RASAT root. “twelve”

The Etymologies list the root ᴹ√RASAT “twelve” and it was long supposed that its unattested derivative *rasta was the Quenya word for “twelve”. In later publications, however, there appeared an attested word yunquë “twelve” (VT47/41) from ✶yūnekē, which is probably to be preferred over *rasta.

The word yunquë dates back to Early Quenya, appearing as ᴱQ. yunqe (PE14/82), so it is possibly that √RASAT and ✶yūnekē at one point coexisted as primitive forms for “twelve”. As a Quenya word, rasta only appears as part of yurasta “twenty four” (twice-twelve) in a discussion of the Elvish duodecimal (base twelve) counting system (PE14/17). It may be that ✶yūnekē was used for the general word for “twelve”, while √RASAT was used for “groups of twelve” as part of this duodecimal system. If so, it may have only been used as a suffix in grouping words in this counting system. That is the theory used in in this lexicon.
