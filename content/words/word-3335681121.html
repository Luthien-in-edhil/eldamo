Q. Poldórëa m. “Valiant; Breaker up of the Hard/Tough”

A late remnant of the original sobriquet of Tulkas, ultimately revised to Astaldo (MR/146). In early writings this name was generally glossed “Valiant” (MR/146, LR/206) or “Strong One” (SM/79) from the root ᴹ√POL(OD) “physically strong” (Ety/POL), but in some late notes Tolkien considered a new etymology of this name with a revised meaning “breaker up of the hard/tough” incorporating the root √DOR “hard” (PE17/181).
